Dear Friends and Partners,

First, thank you. This trip to Pakistan would not have been possible without your prayers and generous support. Because of you, we were able to go, preach the Gospel, train leaders, and see God move in powerful ways.

From February 11–19, our team ministered across several regions of Pakistan. What we witnessed was both deep spiritual hunger and great physical need.

We are also incredibly grateful for God's protection over our team. Just ten days after we left the region, tensions across the Middle East escalated into the current war involving Iran, which has already begun affecting countries throughout the region and causing instability.

We are thankful that the Lord allowed us to complete the mission and return home safely.

---

### Training Pastors and Leaders

One of the most important parts of the trip was investing in local leaders. We hosted two pastors and leaders conferences, equipping pastors and ministry workers with encouragement, biblical teaching, and practical ministry tools.

Many of these leaders serve in extremely difficult conditions. Some travel long distances and face real opposition for preaching the Gospel. Yet their faith and dedication are inspiring. It was a privilege to strengthen and pray for them so they can continue reaching their communities.

---

### Meeting With Government Leadership

During our time in Pakistan, we were also given the opportunity to meet and pray with the Deputy Speaker, who is the first Christian ever to serve in the government of Pakistan.

It was a meaningful moment to stand together in prayer—asking God to give him wisdom, protection, and courage as he represents both his nation and the Christian community within it. Moments like this remind us that God is opening doors in places we never expected.

---

### The Urgent Need for Clean Water

During the trip we traveled to remote villages near the border of India. The conditions there were heartbreaking. Many communities do not have access to clean drinking water.

Families often drink from contaminated sources that lead to disease and sickness. Seeing this firsthand deeply impacted us and reminded us how urgent the need is. Clean water is not just a humanitarian need—it also opens doors for the Gospel and transforms entire villages.

We are now prayerfully considering how we can help bring clean water solutions to some of these communities.

---

### A Street Miracle Event in Karachi

One of the most powerful moments of the trip was a miracle event in Karachi, held right in the middle of a street. Nearly 1,000 people gathered as the Gospel was preached and people came forward for prayer.

What followed was incredible. The presence of God moved in a powerful way, and we began hearing testimony after testimony of healing and transformation.

Here are just a few of the testimonies that came in:

- **Sadiq**, who had been deaf and communicated only through sign language, received prayer and began hearing normally.
- **Adnan**, a 25-year-old man who had been paralyzed and unable to walk, received prayer and began walking again.
- **John**, who had been on dialysis for five years due to a kidney infection, went back to the doctor three days after prayer. The doctor confirmed his kidneys were working and the infection was gone.
- **Sara**, who had a brain tumor, testified that after prayer she returned to the hospital and doctors confirmed she had been healed.
- **Atif**, whose eye had been severely damaged in an accident years ago, testified that God restored his eye during prayer.
- **Ashok**, a Hindu man, was healed from multiple diseases during prayer and publicly gave his life to Jesus.
- A woman who had suffered a fractured arm joint with constant pain for two years was instantly healed when she placed her hand on the joint during prayer.
- A young boy who had been partially blind since birth received prayer and began seeing clearly.
- Another woman testified that kidney stones completely disappeared after prayer.

One of the most powerful testimonies came from a woman who had been suffering from cancer. She came quietly to the crusade and was hesitant to even share her story. After receiving prayer, she later went to the doctor and was told there was no cancer remaining in her body. She said she not only received healing but also gave her life to Jesus that day.

And these are just a few. Our partners on the ground reported that hundreds of testimonies are still being documented and translated.

### Standing on Historic Gospel Ground

While in Karachi, we also had the opportunity to visit the grounds of a YMCA in the heart of the city where decades ago Marilyn Hickey held a large Gospel event that impacted thousands.

Standing there was a powerful reminder of the legacy of those who have gone before us to preach Christ in this nation. As we walked those grounds and prayed together, we began asking the Lord if He might open the door for us to host a large Gospel event there in the future.

We would appreciate your prayers as we seek God's direction for what He may want to do there.
